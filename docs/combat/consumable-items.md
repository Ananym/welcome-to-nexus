# Consumable Items

This page covers the standard consumables everyone should be familiar with.

## Using consumables

While items can be (u)sed with the u key, the fastest way to use consumables is to hold Ctrl + (Inventory item letter).
Items can also be assigned to number keys using the hotkey/macro interface on F11, by opening the interface and then your inventory.

## Vitality

Bear livers can be bought from your path's guild hall, and serve as an effective healing item through the early levels.

Eventually you may want to switch to Life Blossoms, sold in the Bodhi Sanctum on Dae Shore, east of Kugnae. Only registered characters can enter this subpath area - unregistered players may need to ask others to buy blossoms for them. While Life Blossoms don't heal as much per slot as a stack of livers, you can carry as many blossoms as you have inventory slots, letting you stay in caves much longer.

It's a good idea for everyone, especially warriors, to carry emergency healing items. Mountain ginseng fills this role early on, healing 10k vitality per use, and is sold by clan merchants. Eventually 10k vitality is no longer enough to save you, and you will want to swap to Red Potions for a 100% heal sold by Kugnae's alchemist shop in the southeast corner. Red potions are expensive, but much cheaper than recovering from a death! Always keep your emergency healing in a known inventory slot so you don't need to search your inventory while being battered by monsters.

## Mana

Herb pipes are the primary mana restorative, sold at your path's guild hall. Other pipes are available, but nothing provides more mana per slot than basic herb pipes.

Poets and mages will learn "invoke" naturally to supply their mana needs. Rogues will learn "Chance" at ee-san, which lets them invoke. Beyond that, warriors and pre-ee rogues depend on a poet's "inspire" once they outgrow pipes. When a poet is not available, Scrolls of Invocation let anyone invoke. These are crafted by players practicing [Scribing](economy/crafting.md?id=scribing). You can only carry six at once, but they are still recommended if you need to reach your highest level of rage or cunning while solo.

## Status

Scrolls of Protection are also crafted by scribes, and will block curses from creatures such as mythic leaders. They will not cure curses once applied - only a poet can do that. An il-san warrior learns Hoche for the same effect, though this cannot provide 100% uptime. While these can be useful in carnages when used precisely, an enemy's dispel will strip your protection. The right time to use an SoP in the arena is right after being hit with a dispel, delaying the enemy attack just long enough for your team to focus support on you.

Mythic leaders can also poison you. Although it garbles your speech, this isn't a threatening effect, and can be cured with an Indigo potion sold at alchemist shops. Notably, group chat (whispering to '!!') isn't affected, if you need a way to talk through poison.

Green potions can also be found at the alchemist shop, and offer a few minutes of Sanctuary, halving all incoming damage. Likely too expensive for regular use, they can still be handy in a pinch.
